Extreme sea level projections for different global warming levels
Year: 2100
Unit: in meters
The results are based on the datasets and methods from the paper:
Tebaldi, C., Ranasinghe, R., Vousdoukas, M., Rasmussen, D. J., Vega-Westhoff, B., Kirezci, E., . . . Mentaschi, L. (2021). Extreme sea levels at different global warming levels. Nature Climate Change, 11(9), 746-751. doi:10.1038/s41558-021-01127-1

Each CSV file contains 14 columns:
1. Longitude
2. Latitude
3. Lcurrent - lower bound estimate (5th percentile value) of present-day extreme sea level
4. Ccurrent - central estimate (50th percentile value) of present-day extreme sea level
5. Ucurrent - upper bound estimate (95th percentile value) of present-day extreme sea level
6. L1p5degree - lower bound estimate (5th percentile value) of future extreme sea level for 1.5 degree warming level
7. C1p5degree - central estimate (50th percentile value) of future extreme sea level for 1.5 degree warming level
8. U1p5degree - upper bound estimate (95th percentile value) of future extreme sea level for 1.5 degree warming level
9. L3p0degree - lower bound estimate (5th percentile value) of future extreme sea level for 3 degree warming level
10. C3p0degree - central estimate (50th percentile value) of future extreme sea level for 3 degree warming level
11. U3p0degree - upper bound estimate (95th percentile value) of future extreme sea level for 3 degree warming level
9. L5p0degree - lower bound estimate (5th percentile value) of future extreme sea level for 5 degree warming level
10. C5p0degree - central estimate (50th percentile value) of future extreme sea level for 5 degree warming level
11. U5p0degree - upper bound estimate (95th percentile value) of future extreme sea level for 5 degree warming level
